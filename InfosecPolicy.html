Information Security Policy

Touchpoint One, LLC

Purpose: The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to establish a framework to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets within Touchpoint One, LLC.

1. Acceptable Use by Workforce

General Guidelines: All workforce members are required to use Touchpoint One, LLC's information systems, networks, and data responsibly, ethically, and in compliance with all relevant laws, regulations, and organizational policies. Unacceptable use includes, but is not limited to:

  • Unauthorized access to or use of data and systems.
  • Sharing login credentials or allowing unauthorized individuals to access systems.
  • Installing unauthorized software or hardware.
  • Engaging in activities that may compromise the integrity or security of information assets.
  • Using organizational resources for personal or non-business purposes without explicit permission.

Email and Communication Systems: All communication through Touchpoint One, LLC's email and messaging systems must be professional, business-related, and must not contain any content that is discriminatory, harassing, or otherwise inappropriate. The use of personal email accounts for business purposes is strictly prohibited.

Internet Usage: Workforce members must use the internet responsibly and must not engage in illegal activities, access inappropriate content, or download unauthorized software or files that could compromise the security of Touchpoint One, LLC's systems.

Monitoring and Enforcement: Touchpoint One, LLC reserves the right to monitor all network and system activities to ensure compliance with this policy. Any violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.

2. Classification of Data, Its Handling, and Period of Retention

Data Classification: Data must be classified based on its sensitivity and criticality to the organization. The following classification levels are established:

  • Public: Data that is approved for public release and poses no risk if disclosed.
  • Internal: Data that is intended for use within Touchpoint One, LLC and not to be disclosed outside the organization without authorization.
  • Confidential: Sensitive data that requires protection from unauthorized access, including proprietary information, personal data, and financial records.
  • Restricted: Highly sensitive data that requires strict access controls and is only accessible to a limited number of authorized individuals.

Data Handling: Data must be handled according to its classification level:

  • Public: May be freely distributed.
  • Internal: Should be shared only within the organization or with authorized external parties.
  • Confidential: Must be encrypted when stored or transmitted. Access must be restricted to authorized personnel only.
  • Restricted: Must be encrypted and stored in a secure environment. Access is strictly controlled, and all access must be logged and regularly reviewed.

Data Retention Period: Data must be retained in accordance with legal, regulatory, and business requirements:

  • Public: Retain indefinitely unless otherwise specified.
  • Internal: Retain for a minimum of 5 years, or as required by business needs.
  • Confidential: Retain for a minimum of 7 years, or as required by legal or regulatory obligations.
  • Restricted: Retain for the duration of its criticality, with regular reviews to determine if continued retention is necessary. Destroy securely once no longer needed.

Data Disposal: Data must be securely disposed of once it is no longer needed, according to its classification:

  • Public: May be deleted without special procedures.
  • Internal: Must be deleted securely, ensuring it cannot be recovered.
  • Confidential: Must be deleted using secure methods, such as data wiping or physical destruction of media.
  • Restricted: Must be deleted or destroyed using the most secure methods available, with appropriate documentation of the disposal process.
