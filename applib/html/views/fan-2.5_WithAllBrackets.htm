Rules & How to Play A-Game

Bring your A-GAME today.

Have fun, help your team - earn bragging rights, recognition, prizes and A-GAME glory!

  1. When in doubt about what to do - SMILE, put forth your best effort (bring your A-GAME!), assist teammates whenever possible and above all - have fun!
  2. A-GAME games aka "matchups" typically align with two-week or bi-monthly pay periods (though in some cases could span just a week).
  3. At the start of each game the supervisor (coach) selects a starting lineup for the current game.

    Note: To view the agents in the starting lineup for the current of previous games, check your Player Home page or click the Roster or Matchup views in the A-GAME console for this and other team roster details.
  4. Game scoring is based on the aggregate of each starting player's balanced score b which could be multiplied by a factor of 2 or 3 (depending on the position the supervisor has assigned the agent). The team's total score is updated each day and can be monitored in the Matchup view.
  5. At the end of the pay period, a winner is determined and the Win/Loss record for each team is updated and available in the Standings view.
  6. NEW! Position Play. Agents not on the starting lineup are automatically assigned to a position and compete during for recognition and incentives with all other agents assigned to the same position. Check your Player Snapshot to see what position you've been assign to, your rank and other details. Go to Stats b Position Play to view position rankings for other agents.
  7. NEW! A-GAME Xtreme. Xtreme is the A-GAME fantasy-mode that enables executives, HR, Sales, IT, Finance and other personnel to get into the A-GAME action. Xtreme owners draft teams from across contact center operations and compete with other Xtreme teams in a "shadow" fantasy league.
  8. Team Schedules can be viewed in the A-GAME console - Schedule view. Check it out to see what team you're currently competing against and what teams you'll meet head-to-head next.
  9. The Standings view displays the Win/Loss record for your team and other teams in your league division.
  10. Play for Fun, Pride, Recognition and Reward.

Each league is different, but all get into the A-GAME spirit with pre-game events, intra-game promotions, and playoff-season awards and incentives that recognize not only division, conference and league champions, but most improved, most valuable and position-play performance on an individual and team basis.

And if your team doesn't make it to the post-season or gets knocked out early in the playoffs, you're still eligible for ultimate prize b The Medal of Excellence Award (the Big MoE!). The Medal of Excellence is awarded by TouchPoint One to the most valuable and most improved agent-player and supervisor-coach across all corporate leagues based on a combination of 1) your average balanced score throughout the entire A-GAME season, and 2) the extent to which you performed above your normal average prior to the A-GAME season start.

League of Champions Rules
  1. Individual play
    1. All agents play every game.
    2. Agents are auto-assigned to 1 of 10 events per week.
    3. All agents will compete in all 10 events by conclusion of the competition.
    4. The assignment process is randomized to minimize the same group of agents competing against each other in all 10 events.
    5. One Gold medal, one Silver medal and one Bronze medal is awarded for each event per week (30 medals given out per week).
    6. The agent's balance score at the end of the week will be used as the event score.
  2. Team competition
    1. Team standings are based upon the overall medal count of the agents within each team over the course of the competition.
    2. The medal count is based upon 3 points for every gold medal won, 2 points for each silver medal earned and 1 point per bronze medal won.
    3. Ties are broken by the sum of the team's average balance score of each week.
  3. Partner competition
    1. There will be an overall partner competition award at the end of the competition.
    2. Due to the likelihood of partners not being equal in size, the number of teams per partner that will count towards the award will be no more than the lowest team count of all partners. This count will be referred to as the "qualifying team count".
    3. Each team will be assigned a position number based upon the medal count ranking. Ties after the tie breaker will be assigned the same position number for all tied teams.
    4. Once the position numbers are assigned to each team, these position numbers will be added together per partner using only the positions of the number of teams equaling the "qualifying team count". The partner with the lowest score will win. For example:
      1. There are 5 partners competing. 4 partners have 5 teams and 1 partner has 3 teams.
      2. The "qualifying team count" in this example is 3. Therefore, only the top 3 teams' ranking position per partner will be used to determine the final score.
